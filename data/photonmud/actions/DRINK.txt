@USERCLR DRINK Command - Consume Potions or Liquids @THEMECLR
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

@DATACLR Usage:@THEMECLR
  DRINK <item>

@DATACLR What it does:@THEMECLR
  DRINK allows you to consume a potion or liquid from your inventory or
  a container. Drinking potions can heal, cure, or grant magical effects.
  Some liquids may be harmful or have unknown effects.

@DATACLR Rules & Mechanics:@THEMECLR
  - Only drinkable items (potions, water, elixirs) can be used.
  - Potions may restore HP, cure poison, grant buffs, or cause side effects.
  - Drinking is an instant action and does not use a combat turn.
  - Some potions require a saving throw to resist negative effects.

@DATACLR Dice Rolls:@THEMECLR
  - None for beneficial potions.
  - Harmful or cursed potions may require a saving throw (d20 + CON mod).

@DATACLR Outcome:@THEMECLR
  - You gain the potion's effect (heal, buff, cure, etc.).
  - The item is removed from your inventory.
  - If the potion is cursed, you may suffer a penalty.

@DATACLR Class Features:@THEMECLR
  - All classes can drink potions.
  - Clerics and Druids may identify potions more easily.

@DATACLR Tips:@THEMECLR
  - Save healing potions for emergencies.
  - Use IDENTIFY to check unknown potions.
  - Some potions have delayed or hidden effects.

@DATACLR Example:@THEMECLR
  DRINK POTION
    You drink the potion and heal 10 HP.

@DATACLR See also:@THEMECLR
  EAT, INVENTORY, STATUS, IDENTIFY
