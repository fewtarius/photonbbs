@USERCLR BLESS Command - Bless Items or Yourself @THEMECLR
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

@DATACLR Usage:@THEMECLR
  BLESS <item>
  BLESS

@DATACLR What it does:@THEMECLR
  BLESS allows you to bless an item or yourself, removing curses or
  granting temporary bonuses. Clerics and Paladins are most effective
  at blessing, but anyone can attempt it at a temple.

@DATACLR Rules & Mechanics:@THEMECLR
  - Only items in your inventory can be blessed.
  - Blessing removes curses or grants +1 bonus for a short time.
  - Clerics and Paladins have a higher chance of success.
  - Blessing yourself may grant a temporary AC or save bonus.

@DATACLR Dice Rolls:@THEMECLR
  - d20 + WIS mod (+class bonus) vs. DC 15 for success.

@DATACLR Outcome:@THEMECLR
  - Success: Item is blessed or curse is removed.
  - Failure: No effect, or minor penalty if cursed.

@DATACLR Class Features:@THEMECLR
  - Clerics/Paladins: +2 to bless rolls.
  - All classes can attempt to bless at a temple.

@DATACLR Tips:@THEMECLR
  - Bless weapons before major battles.
  - Remove curses before equipping unknown items.

@DATACLR Example:@THEMECLR
  BLESS SHIELD
    Shield is now blessed (+1 AC for 10 turns).

@DATACLR See also:@THEMECLR
  CURSE, IDENTIFY, STATUS
