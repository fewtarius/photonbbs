@USERCLR BEGUILE Command - Attempt to Confuse Target @THEMECLR
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

@DATACLR Usage:@THEMECLR
  BEGUILE <target>

@DATACLR What it does:@THEMECLR
  BEGUILE attempts to confuse or distract a monster or NPC, reducing
  their effectiveness in combat or causing them to ignore you.

@DATACLR Rules & Mechanics:@THEMECLR
  - BEGUILE is used on your turn in combat or outside combat.
  - Roll: d20 + CHA mod; must exceed 16 to succeed.
  - Some monsters/NPCs are immune to beguile.

@DATACLR Dice Rolls:@THEMECLR
  - Beguile Roll: d20 + CHA mod

@DATACLR Outcome:@THEMECLR
  - Success: Target is confused or distracted, may skip a turn or flee.
  - Failure: Target may become enraged or attack.

@DATACLR Class Features:@THEMECLR
  - Bards and high-CHA classes have better chance.
  - All classes can attempt BEGUILE.

@DATACLR Tips:@THEMECLR
  - Use BEGUILE to escape or gain an advantage.
  - Not all targets can be beguiled.

@DATACLR Example:@THEMECLR
  BEGUILE OGRE
    Rolls d20 + CHA mod. If >16, ogre is confused.

@DATACLR See also:@THEMECLR
  CHARM, ATTACK, TALK

@USERCLR Detailed Help for BEGUILE Command @THEMECLR
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

@DATACLR Description:@THEMECLR
  The BEGUILE command allows a player to attempt to confuse or
  distract a target, which can be useful in various situations,
  especially in combat.

@DATACLR Targeting:@THEMECLR
  - You can attempt to BEGUILE any creature or NPC that you can see
  and that has a discernible mind.
  - Some creatures, like undead or constructs, may be immune.

@DATACLR Combat Usage:@THEMECLR
  - Using BEGUILE counts as your action for the turn.
  - If successful, the target may skip their next action or flee
  from you.

@DATACLR Non-Combat Usage:@THEMECLR
  - BEGUILE can also be used outside of combat to escape from
  dangerous situations or to avoid conflict.

@DATACLR Limitations:@THEMECLR
  - BEGUILE has no effect on targets that are already engaged in
  combat with you.
  - The effectiveness of BEGUILE may be reduced if the target is
  already hostile.

@DATACLR Strategy:@THEMECLR
  - Use BEGUILE on stronger or more dangerous foes to turn the
  tide of battle.
  - Consider using BEGUILE to create openings for allies to
  attack or to escape from a losing battle.

@DATACLR Roleplaying:@THEMECLR
  - When using BEGUILE, describe how you attempt to confuse or
  distract the target.
  - Creative descriptions may enhance the effectiveness or
  provide advantages.

@DATACLR Example Scenarios:@THEMECLR
  - BEGUILE an ogre by mimicking its leader's voice, causing it
  to hesitate.
  - Use BEGUILE on a guard to make them forget their
  instructions, creating a diversion.

@DATACLR See also:@THEMECLR
  CHARM, ATTACK, TALK, FLEE
