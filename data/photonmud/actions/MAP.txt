@USERCLR MAP Command - View the World Map @THEMECLR
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

@DATACLR Usage:@THEMECLR
  MAP

@DATACLR What it does:@THEMECLR
  MAP displays a top-down view of the current region, showing your location,
  nearby towns, castles, rivers, mountains, and other landmarks. The map helps
  you navigate the world and plan your journey.

@DATACLR Rules & Mechanics:@THEMECLR
  - The map shows a limited area around your current position.
  - Your position is marked with a special symbol (e.g., @).
  - Major features (towns, castles, lakes, rivers, mountains) are shown with
    unique symbols or colors.
  - Some hidden or secret areas may not appear on the map until discovered.
  - The map does not reveal monsters, objects, or treasures in rooms.

@DATACLR Dice Rolls:@THEMECLR
  - None.

@DATACLR Outcome:@THEMECLR
  - You see a visual representation of your surroundings.
  - Use the map to find routes, avoid obstacles, and locate points of interest.

@DATACLR Class Features:@THEMECLR
  - All classes use MAP the same way.
  - Rangers and Druids may receive extra information about natural features.

@DATACLR Tips:@THEMECLR
  - Use MAP after moving to a new area to orient yourself.
  - Look for roads, bridges, and paths to navigate efficiently.
  - Towns and castles are good places to rest, trade, or find quests.

@DATACLR Example:@THEMECLR
  MAP
    [You see a grid with @ marking your position, T for towns, C for castles.]

@DATACLR See also:@THEMECLR
  LOOK, HERE, STATUS, NEWS
